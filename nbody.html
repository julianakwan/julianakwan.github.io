Cosmological N-body Simulations

Here is a slice of the FLAMINGO simulation, a fully hydrodynamical cosmological simulation that includes over 300 billion interacting fluid elements to represent gas, stars, black holes and dark matter.

Cosmological N-body simulations are Monte-Carlo solutions to the Vlasov-Poisson equation. The simulation volume is split into a number of fluid elements called particles, which represent the different components of the Universe. Massive N-body simulations can take millions of CPU hours to complete because each of these elements will interact with each other via gravity at a minimum; hydrodynamical simulations can also have pressure forces between gas particles.
